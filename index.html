Refugee Analysis


Global Refugee Migration And Patterns

In this project we looked at the total numbers of refugees over the past few years. We wanted to find out which countries account for the highest numbers of refugees, which are the top refugee host countries and what are the refugees’ backgrounds. Even though the amount of data available is limited, we did our best to tell an interesting story with our visualizations.


What is a refugee? A refugee is someone who has been forced to flee his or her country because of persecution, war or violence. A refugee has a well-founded fear of persecution for reasons of race, religion, nationality, political opinion or membership in a particular social group.

What are the world’s top 10 refugee host countries?

  • Turkey (3.7M+)
  • Jordan (2.9M+)
  • Pakistan (1.4M+)
  • Lebanon (1.4M+)
  • Uganda (1.1M+)
  • Germany (1M+)
  • Iran (979’400)
  • Bangladesh (906’600)
  • Ethiopia (921’000)
  • Sudan (908’000)

Out of the 79.5 million people around the world who have fled their homes because of conflict, unrest or disaster, children account for more than half of this total. Child refugees face incredible risks and dangers – including disease, malnutrition, violence, labor exploitation and trafficking. Refugee camps and large reception centers are not safe places for children. We have seen, time and time again, that children without adequate shelter and protection are the most vulnerable to exploitation, abuse and the stress of long-term separation. Without proper support, they are more likely to face lasting educational challenges and social exclusion.

Conclusion:

Our research indicates that the main reasons for refugees fleeing their countries are persecution (national, social, racial, or political), war, economic hardship, hunger. Most refugees flee to a close country because of the economic cost. Comparing the age groups and sex of the refugees, it is clearly visible that more than half of the refugees are under the age of 18. After the age of 60, the amount of refugees drops drastically for both female and male indicating that older people do not have as much incentive to leave their homes.

Refugees are seeking safety, freedom, and a future, which every person deserves. Wealthier countries aren’t doing nearly enough to share the cost of protecting people who have left everything behind. Appeals for humanitarian assistance for refugees are consistently and often severely underfunded. A third of refugees (6.7M people) are hosted by the world’s poorest countries.
